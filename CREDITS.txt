LICENSE ELECTION ADDENDUM
===============================================================================

Purpose
-------
This addendum documents the distributor's chosen license path for third-party
crates that are offered under a dual-license or multi-license choice.

Default election
----------------
For crates whose upstream license expression permits the MIT License as one
available option, the distributor elects to use the MIT License for
redistribution and compliance purposes.

Reason for this election
------------------------
MIT is the lowest-administration path for this dependency set. Its practical
redistribution obligation is to preserve the applicable copyright notice and
permission notice. Apache-2.0 is also permissive, but it brings additional
compliance machinery, especially patent-license terms and NOTICE-file handling
when upstream NOTICE content exists. For "least headache if something happens,"
MIT is the cleaner election when upstream permits it.

For crates licensed as Unlicense OR MIT, the distributor elects MIT. The
Unlicense is permissive, but MIT is more conventional and avoids public-domain
interpretation arguments in jurisdictions where public-domain dedication can be
messier.

For crates licensed as MIT OR Apache-2.0 OR Zlib, the distributor elects MIT.

This election does not relicense the upstream projects, remove upstream notices,
or erase the fact that upstream offers other license paths. It only records the
license path chosen by this distributor. Where an upstream expression uses AND,
every required license remains required.

Election summary
----------------
- MIT OR Apache-2.0: elect MIT.
- Apache-2.0 OR MIT: elect MIT.
- MIT/Apache-2.0: treat as MIT OR Apache-2.0 and elect MIT.
- Unlicense OR MIT: elect MIT.
- MIT OR Apache-2.0 OR Zlib: elect MIT.
- MIT: no election needed; use MIT.
- (MIT OR Apache-2.0) AND Unicode-3.0: elect MIT for the OR branch and also comply with Unicode-3.0. Effective selected basis: MIT AND Unicode-3.0.
